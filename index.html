How I Inspire Rural Youth: Sharing My Mentorship Journey Across Solukhumbu Villages

In the highlands of Solukhumbu, where the mountains touch the clouds and the air hums with quiet resilience, I began my journey as a mentor with a simple belief — that the spark of change lies within every young person. Many rural youths here dream silently, their ambitions often shadowed by limited opportunities. My mission was to awaken those dreams, guide them, and help them find confidence in their own voices. When I started visiting remote villages like Mahakulung, I met young people who were bright, curious, and full of ideas — yet lacked the tools and direction to turn those ideas into action. Through small group discussions, storytelling sessions, and practical training on digital skills and entrepreneurship, I tried to show them that success doesn’t only live in the cities. It can grow right here, in the heart of our communities. One of my proudest moments was organizing a local workshop where we discussed self-reliance and online opportunities. Watching their eyes light up as they realized they could connect to the world from these mountains — that was powerful. Many have since started small initiatives, digital pages, or even community projects inspired by our sessions. But mentorship is not a one-way journey. Every conversation with these youths reminds me of the strength, creativity, and determination that live in rural Nepal. They teach me as much as I teach them — about patience, hope, and the beauty of persistence in the face of uncertainty. Today, my goal remains clear: to build a network of young changemakers who will carry forward the values of transparency, collaboration, and courage. I believe the future of Nepal lies not only in big cities but also in the quiet villages where dreams are being planted — one conversation, one story, and one spark at a time.
